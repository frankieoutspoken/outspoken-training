# Makeup Application Tips

## How to Apply Foundation Without It Looking Cakey
The number one cause of cakey foundation is applying too much product on dry or textured skin. Start with a well-moisturized face and a primer suited to your skin type. Apply foundation in thin layers using a damp beauty sponge — bouncing, not dragging. Build coverage only where you need it (usually around the nose, chin, and under eyes). Set with a light dusting of powder only on your T-zone, not your entire face.

## Smokey Eye for Beginners
Start with a transition shade in your crease — something close to your skin tone but slightly darker. Use a fluffy brush and blend in windshield-wiper motions. Then apply a darker shade to the outer corner and along the lash line. The key is blending: spend more time blending than applying. Finish with a shimmer shade on the center of the lid for dimension. ## How to Contour Your Face Shape
Contouring is about creating shadow and light. Apply a matte shade one to two shades darker than your skin in the hollows of your cheeks, along your jawline, and on the sides of your nose. Apply highlighter on the high points: tops of cheekbones, bridge of nose, cupid's bow, and brow bone. Blend everything with a damp sponge for a natural finish. ## Making Lipstick Last All Day
Exfoliate your lips first with a gentle scrub or soft toothbrush. Apply a thin layer of lip balm and let it absorb for a few minutes. Line your lips with a matching lip liner, filling in the entire lip for a base. Apply lipstick, blot with a tissue, and apply a second coat. ## Dealing with Blemish-Prone Skin
Choose non-comedogenic products labeled "oil-free" or "won't clog pores." Use a color-correcting concealer (green cancels redness) before your regular concealer.
